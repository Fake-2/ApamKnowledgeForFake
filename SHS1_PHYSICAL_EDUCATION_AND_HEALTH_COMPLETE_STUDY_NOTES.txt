The SHS Physical Education and Health curriculum aims to equip learners with 
knowledge, skills, and competencies to:
- Understand career pathways in PE and Health
- Engage in physical activity through traditional dances
- Develop understanding of health and wellness
- Master basic gymnastics for fitness and skill development

KEY LEARNING OUTCOMES:
- Identify and discuss career pathways in PE and Health
- Explain traditional dances as physical and recreational activities
- Understand health pillars and wellness concepts
- Perform basic gymnastics activities (forward roll, backward roll, handstand)

WEEKS 3-6: TRADITIONAL DANCES FOR HEALTH & FITNESS

1. KEY DEFINITIONS

TRADITIONAL DANCE
- Dance depicting life of a group in a particular community, region, or country
- Accompanied by strong local musical forms linked to local beliefs
- Sequence of body movements with aesthetic and symbolic values

DANCE CONCEPTS (Basic Elements)
- BODY: Dancers use their body to express themselves
- ACTION: The movements performed
- SPACE: Where the body generates movements
- TIME: Temporal element of movements
- ENERGY: Intensity and quality of movements
- All characterized by rhythmic movement and varying intensity

PHYSICAL ACTIVITY
- Any bodily movement produced by skeletal muscles requiring energy
- Forms: dancing, walking, jogging, running, cycling, swimming, etc.

RECREATIONAL ACTIVITY
- Experiences/organized activities undertaken for fun during free time
- Includes activities requiring physical activity, interactions, playing
- Examples: dancing, games, swimming, hiking, gardening

TRADITIONAL DANCE AS PHYSICAL & RECREATIONAL ACTIVITY
- Combines cultural expression with bodily movement
- Provides fun while maintaining good health and fitness
- Serves dual purpose: cultural preservation and physical fitness

2. LINK BETWEEN TRADITIONAL DANCE & PHYSICAL/RECREATIONAL ACTIVITIES

1. Engages whole body and broad group of muscles (like physical exercise)
2. Makes body flexible and aids motion (like physical exercise)
3. Achieves aerobic exercise through moving, jumping, twirling
4. Provides exercise, fun, and entertainment simultaneously

3. HEALTH BENEFITS OF ENGAGING IN TRADITIONAL DANCE

✓ Improves coordination, muscular strength, endurance, motor fitness, balance
✓ Improves cardiovascular health
✓ Promotes greater flexibility
✓ Creates sense of belonging and social interaction
✓ Improves communication and creativity
✓ Promotes critical thinking
✓ Increases understanding and appreciation of other cultures
✓ Improves weight loss
✓ Relieves stress

4. EXAMPLES OF TRADITIONAL DANCES IN GHANA

Bima, Agbadza, Kpanaliumni, Kete, Kple, Kundum, Boboobo, Apatampa, Zigi, 
Atsiagbekor, Adowa, Bamaya, Kpanlogo, Pogne

5. SPECIFIC TRADITIONAL DANCES COVERED

AGBADZA (Ewe Dance - Volta Region)
Origin: Derived from old war dance called Atrikpui
History: Created on battlefield to inspire warriors, ease fears, tell of 
         ancestors' struggles at Notsie
Characteristics: Both slow and quick movements
Performance Context: Festivals, naming ceremonies, marriages, religious 
                     activities, graduations, funerals
Steps Involved:
1. Banyinyi - Short introductory prayer to gods and ancestors
2. Vutsortsor - Main dance with clapping and singing
3. Adzo - Less energetic; only master drums with gankogui and ahatse

KPANLOGO (Ga Dance - Greater Accra Region)
Origin: Emerged in late 1960s as innovative form influenced by American rock 
        and roll
Original Name: Gbajo (meaning "storytelling" in Ga)
History: Gave younger Ga generations distinction from elders post-independence
        Now performed nationwide during all occasions by all ages
Steps Involved:
1. Dancers start with swinging of arms
2. Arms movements followed by rapid torso movement
3. Torso movement followed by quick footwork
4. Footwork followed by spinning of waist

ADOWA (Akan Dance - Multiple Regions)
Origin: Movements originated from antelope movements (Adowa in Twi language)
Legend: Queen mother Abrewa Tutuwa of Ashanti fell ill; required antelope 
        slaughter for healing. Warriors mimicked captivating antelope movements 
        to celebrate her recovery. Later, older women imitated and improved moves.
Performance Context: Festivals, funerals, engagements, welcoming visitors, 
                     celebrations, durbars, ruler installations
Characteristics: Mainly uses hands and feet
Steps Involved:
1. Apply pressure and move right foot forward
2. Flung or stretch out arms
3. Lift head back and arch or twist body
4. High kicks, leaps, and measured walks
Note: Dancers ensure interaction with drummer using whole body

6. INTEGRATION OF CONTEMPORARY DANCE WITH TRADITIONAL DANCE
- Incorporates modern dance movement patterns into traditional dances
- Enhances participation and interest
- Maintains cultural authenticity while adapting to modern context

WEEKS 7-8: HEALTH, WELLNESS & HEALTHY LIFESTYLE

1. CONCEPT OF HEALTH

Definition (WHO 1947):
"Health is a state of complete physical, mental and social well-being and 
not merely the absence of disease or infirmity"

Alternative Definition:
Process of discovering (diagnosing diseases, risk factors, injuries) and 
using/protecting all resources within bodies, minds, spirits, families, 
communities, and environment

Health emphasizes optimum functioning of physical body, considering:
- What one eats (NUTRITION)
- How much is eaten
- When it is eaten
- How the body moves (EXERCISE)
- Absence of severe or long-lasting disease conditions

2. PILLARS OF HEALTH (4 Key Pillars)

PILLAR 1: PHYSICAL EXERCISE

Definition: All voluntary movement of body including walking, cycling, 
           active recreation, play at any skill level for enjoyment

Types: All activities at any intensity performed any time of day, including:
- Structured exercise
- Incidental activities: walking stairs, shopping, washing car, dancing, 
  sweeping, weeding integrated into daily routines

Benefits of Regular Physical Exercise:
✓ Manage weight
✓ Reduce risk of non-communicable diseases (heart diseases, diabetes)
✓ Strengthen bones and muscles
✓ Improve ability to carry out everyday activities
✓ Reduce stress
✓ Reduce depression and anxiety

PILLAR 2: SLEEP AND RELAXATION

Definition of Sleep: Period of rest with reduced mental/physical activity, 
                     altered consciousness, withdrawn bodily activity

Recommended Duration: 7-8 hours every night

Changes During Sleep:
- Decrease in muscle activity
- Greatly reduced interactions with environment
- Restoration of body and mind

Benefits:
✓ Reduce tiredness
✓ Improve brain function and memory
✓ Lessen anger and frustration
✓ Facilitate healing

Relaxation Activities: Music listening, meditation, leisure activities

PILLAR 3: NUTRITION

Definition: Process of providing/obtaining food necessary for health and body growth

Key Terms:
- DIET: Food and drink individuals select to consume
- NUTRIENTS: Chemical compounds in food crucial for functioning and growth
- FOOD: Fuel for bodies; eating well promotes overall health

Factors Contributing to Healthy Eating:
1. Choose foods based on health benefits
2. Add more fruits and vegetables to diet daily
3. Eat fruits and vegetables that are in season
4. Increase daily intake: whole grains, fat-free/low-fat milk products
5. Buy local, fresh, good quality ingredients
6. Cook from scratch; avoid takeaways/fast food where possible

Benefits of Good Nutrition:
- Supports physical development
- Prevents disease
- Enhances energy and mental clarity
- Promotes long-term health

PILLAR 4: HYDRATION (WATER)

Definition: Clear liquid with no color, taste, or smell used for drinking, 
           cooking, washing

Standard Benchmark: 2 liters (8 glasses) daily
(May vary depending on season or work/activity nature)

Benefits of Drinking Water:
✓ Aids in digestion
✓ Helps body absorb important nutrients from food
✓ Gets rid of poisons and waste through urine, sweat, bowel movements
✓ Hydrates skin, fights aging and wrinkles naturally
✓ Helps maintain normal body temperature
✓ Helps lubricate and cushion joints
✓ Helps circulate food nutrients around body
✓ Helps prevent constipation
✓ Helps prevent kidney stones

Importance: Do not wait until thirsty before drinking

3. WELLNESS

Definition 1: Attitude enabling people to develop healthy habits daily to 
             attain better physical and mental health

Definition 2: Active process through which individuals become aware of themselves 
             and what can positively/negatively affect them, making informed 
             choices for more successful existence

Definition 3: Achieved by practicing healthy lifestyle, making choices/lifestyle 
             changes, controlling/eliminating unhealthy behaviors

4. HEALTHY LIFESTYLE

Definition: Actions or behaviors that reduce one's risk of diseases, 
           accidents, and enhance wellness

Examples of Healthy Lifestyle Factors:
1. Practice personal hygiene
2. Practice safe sex
3. Socialize with supportive friends and family
(Learners encouraged to add more examples)

5. UNHEALTHY LIFESTYLE

Definition: Actions or behaviors that increase person's likelihood of 
           developing illness, injuries, and even death

Examples of Unhealthy Lifestyle Factors:

1. DRINKING ALCOHOL
   Consequences: Liver damage, stroke, heart disease, cancer

2. SMOKING
   Consequences: Lung cancer, respiratory conditions, dental problems, 
                vision loss, cardiovascular diseases

3. UNPROTECTED SEX
   Consequences: Contraction and spread of sexually transmitted diseases/infections

4. SELF-MEDICATION
   Definition: Taking medicines without doctor's prescription
   Consequences: Wrong medication for right condition, incorrect diagnosis, 
                incorrect dosage, medication misuse

5. PROLONGED SCREEN TIME
   Definition: Excessive sitting and viewing TV, computer, or phone use
   Consequences: Eye strain, headaches, neck/shoulder pain, dry eyes, 
                blurred vision, near-sightedness, sedentary lifestyle, 
                increased obesity risk and related health issues

6. IMPORTANCE OF LIVING A HEALTHY LIFESTYLE

1. Prevention of Diseases
   - Reduces risk of various diseases including hereditary conditions
   - Creates protective barrier against illness

2. Saves Money
   - Reduces healthcare expenses
   - Good annual check-ups reveal health status early
   - Prevention is more economical than treatment

3. Lengthens Lifespan
   - Basic healthy lifestyle habits connected with longer life
   - Quality of life improvements

SECTION 4: GYMNASTICS
================================================================================

WEEKS 9-11: BASIC GYMNASTICS FLOOR ACTIVITIES FOR FITNESS

1. DEFINITION AND CONCEPT OF GYMNASTICS

Definition: Systematic sports performance combining flexibility, balance, 
           endurance, coordination, strength, and agility

Components: Acrobatic skills (jumps, flips, rolls, turns)

Types of Gymnastics (5 Main Types):
- Artistic Gymnastics
- Rhythmic Gymnastics
- Trampoline Gymnastics
- Acrobatics
- Aerobics

Scope: For SHS purposes, only floor activities covered (performed on floor/mat)

Health Benefits of Gymnastics:
- Develops arms, legs, shoulders, back, chest muscles
- Improves flexibility and coordination
- Enhances balance and strength
- Develops motor skills and body awareness

2. FLOOR ACTIVITIES - FORWARD ROLL

DEFINITION & CONCEPT:
Activity performed by squatting, tucking head, rolling over body in forward 
circle on floor, standing up at end

PURPOSE: Improves skill, health-related fitness, coordination, and balance

STARTING POSITION (Prosper Stance):
1. Squat in front of mat with feet together
2. Place hands on mat/foam in front with elbows bent
3. Fingers pointing straight forward
4. Hands evenly spaced at shoulder width
5. Head tucked in between arms
6. Chin touching chest

THE ROLL - Step-by-Step:
1. Tuck head in with chin to chest
2. Raise hips and look backwards through legs
3. Bend knees
4. Lower head and initiate roll by pushing off with feet
5. Keep head tucked - AIM to land on UPPER BACK, NOT neck
6. Keep arms straight initially as roll starts
7. Gradually bend arms to absorb body weight impact
8. Roll over slowly
9. Ask teammates for support if needed

FINISHING POSITION:
1. Complete roll by ending in squatting position
2. Stand up WITHOUT supporting body with hands
3. Raise arms above head after standing
4. Maintain straightened legs after roll completes
5. Support learners finding difficulty in finishing

SAFETY CONSIDERATION: Protect neck at all times - roll on upper back

3. FLOOR ACTIVITIES - BACKWARD ROLL

DEFINITION & CONCEPT:
Rocking back movement of body by placing hands on shoulders (palms facing 
upwards), rolling in one motion by bringing feet over head, lifting hips, 
pressing hands into mat, finishing in standing position

PURPOSE: Develops balance, coordination, and body control

STARTING POSITION (Stand/Squat Stance):
1. Stand with back straight
2. Knees and thighs together, parallel to floor
3. Hands held just above shoulders with palms facing upwards
4. Perform squat/crouch action

THE ROLL - Step-by-Step:
1. Slowly lean back until back touches mat/foam
2. Tuck head and chin to chest
3. Maintain rounded back
4. Swing legs backwards over head
5. As feet touch floor, push off floor with hands
6. Bring legs up over head, pushing with hands/arms just before feet contact floor
7. Move into squat stand with support (if needed)

FINISHING POSITION:
1. Roll backwards, bringing legs up over head
2. Push with hands and arms just before feet contact floor
3. Move into squat stand
4. Straighten up and raise arms above head to finish
5. Transition from squat to standing position

SAFETY NOTES:
- Proper hand positioning prevents neck strain
- Rounded back position critical for safety
- Support from peers or teacher recommended initially

4. FLOOR ACTIVITIES - HANDSTAND

DEFINITION & CONCEPT:
Fundamental body position where gymnast/individual supports entire body 
upside down, balancing on hands and arms with body held straight and vertical

KEY TECHNIQUE: KICK-UP
Process used to transition from standing to handstand position
- Kick one leg upwards
- Simultaneously push off ground with other leg
- Generates energy to lift body into inverted position

MOVEMENTS INVOLVED:
- Short step taken forward
- Back leg swung up behind
- Shift body weight from feet to hands
- Bring both legs tight together
- Use fingers and heels of hands to maintain balance

STARTING POSITION (Lunge Entry):
1. Start in lunge position
2. One leg forward (in front) with bent knee
3. One leg extended back
4. Body upright and ready

STEP-BY-STEP PROGRESSION:

Step 1: STARTING POSITION - Lunge Position
- Front leg bent slightly
- Back leg extended
- Arms at sides

Step 2: RAISE ARMS
- Raise both arms straight up over head
- Point fingers upwards
- Biceps beside ears
- Keep arms straight (without locking elbows)

Step 3: REACH DOWN
- Reach down towards floor with both hands
- Lift back leg simultaneously
- Keep arms and back straight and firm
- Bend at waist
- Place palms flat on floor
- Shift weight forward onto front leg
- Pick up back leg
- Body forms straight line from shoulders to toes of back leg
- Spread fingers wide for stable base

Step 4: KICK UP
- Kick up onto hands forcefully
- Bring both legs together above head
- Swing back leg straight up behind (like kicking something up)
- Front leg follows naturally
- Bring body up

Step 5: MAINTAIN BALANCE
- Keep body as straight and rigid as possible
- Balance using fingers and heels of hands
- Once balancing point found, maintain position

FINISHING POSITION:
1. Lower back leg down by bending knees
2. Bring knees towards chest, OR
3. Slowly lower legs straight down
4. Gradually raise body up to standing position
5. Raise arms up above head to finish

MODIFICATIONS & SUPPORT:
- Handstand can first be performed AGAINST A WALL
- Can be supported by partner initially
- Wheelbarrow race and frog stand good preparatory activities

PREPARATORY ACTIVITIES:

WHEELBARROW RACE
- Pair activity
- One person acts as "wheelbarrow" (on hands)
- Partner holds their legs as "pusher"
- Race within demarcated area to finish line
- Develops coordination, balance, and teamwork

FROG STAND (Crow Pose)
- Balance on hands with knees resting on back of upper arms
- Arms bent, hands firmly on ground shoulder-width apart
- Body weight supported on hands
- Legs lifted off ground
- Creates stable, balanced crouching position
- Preparatory to full handstand

SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS:
- Always have support/spotting initially
- Use mats/foam for safety
- Proper supervision essential
- Peer assistance recommended
- Progression from wall support to free-standing
- Adequate warm-up before attempting

COMMON EXAM QUESTIONS & ANSWERS
================================================================================

Q1: Explain why it is important to choose a career that aligns with one's 
    interests, skills, and passions.

A: Choosing a career aligned with interests, skills, and passions is important 
because it leads to:
- Job satisfaction and happiness in professional life
- Motivation and drive to excel
- Increased productivity and better performance
- Long-term commitment and engagement
- Resilience to overcome challenges
- Greater job opportunities through excellence
- Positive impact contributing to causes you care about
- Enhanced creativity and innovation
- Better financial prospects

Q2: Describe the link between traditional dance and physical/recreational 
    activity.

A: Traditional dance links to physical and recreational activity through:
1. Engages whole body using broad muscle groups (physical exercise benefit)
2. Makes body flexible and aids motion (like physical training)
3. Achieves aerobic exercise through moving, jumping, twirling (cardiovascular)
4. Provides simultaneous exercise, fun, and entertainment
5. Creates social interaction and sense of belonging
6. Preserves cultural heritage while promoting fitness

Q3: What are the four pillars of health?

A: The four pillars of health are:
1. Physical Exercise - voluntary body movement for fitness and well-being
2. Sleep and Relaxation - 7-8 hours nightly for restoration and healing
3. Nutrition - balanced diet with fruits, vegetables, whole grains
4. Hydration - drinking 2 liters (8 glasses) of water daily

Q4: Describe the proper body positions for performing a forward roll.

A: Forward roll proper positions:

Starting Position:
- Squat in front of mat with feet together
- Place hands on mat with elbows bent, fingers pointing forward
- Hands at shoulder width
- Head tucked between arms with chin on chest

During Roll:
- Keep head tucked (ESSENTIAL for safety)
- Keep upper back rounded
- Land on upper back (NOT neck)
- Keep arms initially straight, then bend to absorb weight
- Legs kept straight with toes pointed until roll end

Finishing Position:
- End in squatting position
- Stand up WITHOUT hand support
- Straighten legs completely
- Raise arms above head

Q5: Explain why sitting for prolonged screen time is unhealthy.

A: Prolonged screen time is unhealthy because it causes:
- Eye strain and dry eyes
- Headaches and neck/shoulder pain
- Blurred vision and potential near-sightedness
- Sedentary lifestyle leading to physical inactivity
- Increased obesity risk and related health issues
- Reduced physical activity and social interaction
- Postural problems from improper sitting
- Mental health impacts including anxiety

Q6: Describe the health benefits of engaging in traditional dance.

A: Traditional dance provides health benefits through:
1. Improved coordination, strength, endurance, motor fitness, and balance
2. Enhanced cardiovascular health through aerobic activity
3. Increased flexibility and mobility
4. Created sense of belonging and social connection
5. Improved communication and creative expression
6. Promoted critical thinking and cultural appreciation
7. Facilitated weight loss through physical exertion
8. Stress relief and mental health support
9. Development of cultural identity and pride
