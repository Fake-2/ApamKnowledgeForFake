A LETTER LONG OVERDUE  ·  WRITTEN IN TRUTH  ·  SEALED IN REGRET

JANICE

Aseda Nhyra Abbey — forever called Jarvis

I built this page because every time I opened my mouth,
the words came out wrong — or never came out at all.
This was the only way I could say it properly.

SCROLL TO READ
CHAPTER ONE

You Were Real.
And I Treated You Like You Weren't.

Janice Aseda Nhyra Abbey — Jarvis — I have been carrying these words far longer than I deserved to. You were someone who chose to be around me, and instead of cherishing that, I turned it into something selfish. I touched you for my own pleasure when you deserved to be held with reverence. I was cold and distant when you deserved warmth and full presence. I used you when you deserved to be chosen — deliberately, openly, and with care.

She stood like a lantern in the dark of who I was,
and I — I kept blowing out her flame
because I was afraid her light
would show me who I truly am.

And I did not like what lived in that mirror.
So I put out the light
and called the darkness comfortable.

There is no excuse that would be enough. But I owe you the truth — not the version I wore to protect myself. The real one. The one that admits I was wrong in every way, at every single turn, and that you deserved someone who knew better — or at least, someone willing to try.

CHAPTER TWO

The Things I Did
That I Cannot Undo

CONFESSION · I
I was evil towards you. Not passively — deliberately. I chose cruelty over kindness again and again, watched it hurt you, and kept going. That is not confusion. That is selfishness at its worst.
CONFESSION · II
I touched you for my own pleasure when what you needed was someone who wanted to know you — to date you, hold you gently, court you with intention. You deserved all of that. I took what I wanted and kept you at a distance.
CONFESSION · III
I lied. Constantly. About big things and small ones — and worst of all, about what you meant to me. I denied that I ever loved you, even while something inside me screamed that it wasn't true. Every word was a coward's shield.
CONFESSION · IV
I knew you were hurting. I was never blind to it. I chose to pretend otherwise. I played dumb, acted unbothered — while knowing exactly what I was doing to you. That is the version of me I am most ashamed of.
CONFESSION · V
Everything I did to hurt you was intentional and selfish. I cannot soften that. Every act of coldness, every lie, every moment I treated you as less than you were — I chose all of it. That truth lives in me.
CONFESSION · VI
I wore a mask for so long I stopped knowing who lived beneath it. I showed up as a character — distant, untouchable — when deep down I was terrified of being truly seen. You could see through it. And that terrified me most.
I AM SORRY.

Not for what I might lose. For what I put you through.

CHAPTER THREE

I Was Scared of You
Because You Were More Consistent Than Me

Here is the truth I buried while it lived rent-free in my chest every night: I loved you. Past tense only because I broke what could have been — not because the feeling was never real. It was real. You were real. And I treated real like it was something to be managed rather than something to be grateful for.

You were too good. Too warm. Too present. Something in me — the part that had never learned to simply receive something good without destroying it — was terrified that if I let you in completely, you would eventually see how broken I actually was and leave. So I made sure you left first.

The bravest thing she ever did
was love someone who refused to be loved.
The saddest thing I ever did
was refuse her — and call it strength.

Fear dressed in armor still bleeds.
It just bleeds inward,
where no one can witness it
but the one who chose the wound.

The fear of being with someone more consistent than me crept into every interaction we had. And instead of working on myself to deserve you, I made myself deserve less of you. It was cowardice dressed as indifference. I am not proud of a single second of it, Janice.

CHAPTER FOUR

What You Didn't Know
Was Happening Behind the Silence

Every time you ignored me — and you had every right to — I did not move on unbothered the way I performed. I went to Sylvia. I asked what I was doing wrong. I listened. I tried to understand. I tried to fix it. Because somewhere inside the mess of who I was, I cared about you more than I ever let you see.

THE THING ABOUT CARE
Every time you pulled away, something in me quietly panicked. It was proof — even to me — that I was not as indifferent as I claimed to be. I just didn't know how to turn that panic into something that looked like love rather than the opposite.
WHAT I SHOULD HAVE SAID
I should have been brave enough to say — "I like you. I am scared. But I choose you over the fear." Instead I swallowed that sentence and handed you cruelty. I called it protecting myself when really I was protecting a broken version of me that didn't have to be vulnerable.
WHAT YOU ALWAYS DESERVED
A man who showed up for you. Who was honest about his feelings. Who took you on dates instead of taking advantage. Who chose you openly, proudly, consistently — not because he was afraid of losing you, but because you were worth choosing every single day.
FINAL WORDS

I Will Always Remember You,
Janice Aseda.

You were someone who brought me genuine joy — real joy — and I returned it with maltreatment. With coldness. With lies. With a version of myself I do not recognize anymore and do not wish to be again.

I will remember you
not as a chapter I am glad is over,
but as the one I wish I had read better
when I had the pages in my hands.

You deserved someone present.
Someone honest.
Someone worthy.

I was none of those things.
And I am deeply, genuinely,
irreversibly sorry.

You are Janice Aseda Nhyra Abbey — Jarvis — and you were never the problem. Never too much. Never not enough. Simply deserving of far more than what I gave.

I know forgiveness is earned, not demanded.
I am not asking you to forget. I am only asking you to know
that this apology is real — and that I built this page
because it was the only way I could say it properly.

WITH EVERYTHING I AM CAPABLE OF MEANING —
Frederick Atiase Kodjo Eli
